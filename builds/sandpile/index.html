What you're seeing: Grains of sand drop on a grid. When a cell reaches height 4, it topples — sending one grain to each neighbor. Those neighbors may topple too, creating a cascade (avalanche).

The surprise: Without any tuning, the system evolves to a critical state where avalanche sizes follow a power law: P(s) ~ s-τ with τ ≈ 1.2. Tiny and huge avalanches coexist — no characteristic scale.
